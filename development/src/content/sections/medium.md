# Typography & Headers

This section demonstrates the typographic hierarchy and header styling used throughout the application. It serves as a visual test for `H1` through `H6` tags.

## Level 2 Header

This is a standard level 2 header, often used for major sections within a document. It should be prominent but distinct from the main title.

### Level 3 Header

Level 3 headers are used for subsections. They provide structure without dominating the visual flow.

#### Level 4 Header

Smaller subsections use level 4. These are useful for grouping related paragraphs.

##### Level 5 Header

Rarely used, but supported. Level 5 headers are often styled similarly to bold text or small caps.

###### Level 6 Header

The smallest header level. Usually reserved for minor labels or low-level structural elements.
